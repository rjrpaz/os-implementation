Course Description:  This course covers relationships among computer components, structures and systems, hardware features, costs, capabilities and selection. It also includes the principles of operating systems and the inter-relationships between the operating system and the architecture of computer systems.

Honor Code:  By accepting admission to Radford University, each student makes a commitment to understand, support, and abide by the University Honor Code without compromise or exception.  Violations of academic integrity will not be tolerated.   This class will be conducted in strict observance of the Honor Code.  Refer to your Student Handbook for details.  In this class, the student is expected to do all out-of-class assignments on their own, without help from any other students.  Of course, the team project is the exception to this and is discussed below.  

Homework & Discussions:  Homework will consist mostly of questions from the end-of-chapter questions from the texts.  In-class discussions will follow from the chapter questions and in-class lectures.  These discussions are planned initially to be small group (breakout) discussions in separate breeze "meeting rooms."  Discussants will be assigned rooms;  one member will be designated as a coordinator, who is responsible for making sure discussants cooperate and coordinate so that no single person dominates and everyone has the opportunity to speak (This is why headsets are required.) as well as, most importantly, the discussions are recorded.  I (the instructor) will join the breakout discussions to make sure all is going well and to determine who is participating.

Programming:  There are 2 programming projects.  One is a Command Line Interpreter, like a UNIX shell.  A user can type in a UNIX command and your program will execute that command.  (NOTE:  We will discuss at length what this entails.)  This first program needs to run under the UNIX OS.  The second program will be a large project simulating the system scheduler for an Operating system.  These projects are subject to change.

Exams:  Exam questions will come from the end-of-chapter questions and class discussions.   Course lectures will cover selected parts of the chapters, but you are required to read, study, and understand all of the assigned material & chapter questions in the text.   You will be preparing most of your "study sheet" for exams while doing the chapter homework.

Attendance: Attendance is essential to performing well in this course because in-"class" discussions will drive the course.  Therefore, attendance is required. Attendance will be taken and will directly affect your grade. You may miss 1  class for "free";  this free miss includes any and all days that you are not in class, including days that you are sick (unless you develop an extended illness or have a personal/family catastrophe).  Every missed class after that 3 will lower your grade by 3% of your final grade. You will be counted late if you show up more than 5 minutes late;  absent if you show up for class more than 15 minutes late.  Habitual lateness will also lower your grade.
